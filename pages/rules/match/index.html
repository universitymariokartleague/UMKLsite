Match Rules

Please visit the FAQs page for questions about the bot and server
16/06/2025 - updated disconnection rules

1. Teams and Matches

1.1.Tracks may only be played once during a match. If a team picks a track that has already been played and that gets chosen by the roulette, they will receive a -30 point penalty. If the track was repicked via random, no penalty will be applied. If a random track is repicked multiple times, no penalty will be applied.
1.1.1.For any successive repicks, the penalty will be increased by 30 points; for example, if a team repicks twice, they will receive a -60 penalty on their second repick, for a -90 points deduction in total.
1.2.Teams must play the full amount of matches available to them.
1.2.1.Each team will play a series of matches, consisting of 12 races against their opponents, in a 6v6 format.
1.2.2.The teams that are playing in a season are decided before it starts - new teams cannot be admitted during a season's run.
1.3.A team must confirm their lineup before their scheduled match. All players in the team's lineup must 'Sign Up' through their respective announcement channels. The lineup must not be changed after the scheduled start time.
Please visit the FAQs for instructions on the Cheep Cheep app.
1.4.The members of a team must use an agreed upon clan tag (identifying characters at the start of a player's name, eg: ¥★zy) on their Switch profile's name. The display name with the clan tag should be applied before the match starts.
Please note that the character limit for a Switch profile's name is 10.
1.4.1.The clan tag must be the same for each member of the team, and should be used throughout the entire match.
1.4.2.Differing teams cannot use the same clan tag, including strings of uppercase and lowercase of the same letters or similar looking characters, and additionally hiragana and katakana tags of the same sound. The matching characters should be placed before the display name, so make sure your casing and separators are the same across your team.
Below are some examples:
¥★zy, G☆DUGO is allowed
MKS Primo, KCG Cait is allowed
KCG|SRa1, KCG Cait is not allowed (differing separator)
Hebidae MKS, Skipp BHM is not allowed (tag is after display name)
あ★hataraku, ア★konota is not allowed (あ and ア have the same sound)
1.5.Teams must be present for each of their scheduled matches.
1.5.1.After the room has been made, the races will start no later than 15 minutes after the scheduled time.
Please check the scheduled times on the matches page or via Discord events.
1.5.2.All matches are 6v6 by default. Teams with only 5 players available must play 5v6 by default. 5v5 (or a lesser format) is not allowed unless stated otherwise by the board.
1.5.3.Failure to get at least 5 players in the room at the scheduled time will result in a 0-493 score in the opponent's favor and a potential disqualification from the event.
1.6.If a player disconnects during a match, the score of the AI racer on that race will be counted towards that racers total points. (See 3.1 in case more players disconnect).
1.7.Please screenshot the results screen after every race and at the end of the match, it helps to solve any issues that may arise (disconnections, points needing to be added, etc.)
1.8.In the event of teams being tied on points at the end of a season, their highest finishing positions will be compared to determine their ranking.

2. Hosting

2.1.A host, who will be responsible for setting up the lobby with the correct rules and sharing the Room ID, will be picked. Players can volunteer to be hosts, and the selected host should be based on criteria in 2.2.
2.2.Ideally, a host with an ethernet connection and an unrestricted NAT type (type A or B) should be used, NAT Type Info (Nintendo).
2.3.The host should start the room with the following race settings:
Class: 150cc
Teams: No Teams
Items: Normal Items
CPU: Hard CPU
CPU Vehicles: All Vehicles
Race Count: 12 Races

3. Disconnections and Lag

3.1.If a player disconnects before the first race starts (by the end of the Lakitu Countdown), the room must be restarted. This rule only applies once, so a subsequent disconnection of this nature will be ignored and the room will not be closed.
3.2.If 3 or more players disconnect or experience item lag (delayed items consisting of only coins or shrooms), the race will not count and the room should be reopened. Please alert the #match-chat channel if this occurs.
3.3.If a race does not finish due to lag, the race will not be counted and the room will be reopened.
3.4.When disconnections occur, you must use the /report-disconnection command, and also capture the disconnection (or take a photo with another device if this isn't possible), posting it in the #match-chat channel, signifying that you have disconnected. This is to help better inform teams about what actions to take when many disconnections occur, like a change of host or replaying a race. Disconnections and following evidence must be reported for these remedies to be considered.
3.5.If the above rules are triggered, a change of host may be requested.

4. Disqualifications

4.1.If there is a player on the university's team that is not a member (current students, alumni, university faculty and prospective students) of said university, they are disqualified from that match and the team will not receive any points for that match (the same point rules as forfeiting mentioned in 1.5.3 apply).
4.2.We reserve the right to disqualify a team for intentional disconnections, ragequitting or other problematic behaviour.
4.3.For other blatant offenses (cross-teaming, hacking, etc.) disqualifications will also apply.
4.4.If repeated violations occur, the board reserves the right to remove that team from the entire UMKL. Board members cannot review infractures relating to their own team.
